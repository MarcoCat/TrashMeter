PickUp3 is an easy, fun way for each of us to help protect marine life and ocean health.

PickUp3. Simple action. Instant impact.

When we hear the ocean is in trouble it’s easy to feel overwhelmed. If you want to take action but don’t know where to start, PickUp3 is your answer.

PickUp3 is a growing movement driven by communities that care. These communities want to remove plastic and other waste from our beaches, educate on the importance of ocean health and inspire people to take action and care for their oceans.

How to record your garbage count:

  1. Use the camera app on your mobile phone to scan the QR code on event posters or participating beach garbage cans.
  2. Click on the link that pops up in the image.
  3. Enter in your trash count on the web page!

If you can’t find the QR code, don’t worry, you can still contribute!

Visit pickup3.org/trash-meter/ and select (or enter) the beach name and number of pieces of garbage you collected from the beach.

Every week we will update our trash meter on the home page so you can see how your simple actions have directly impacted your local beach!

Don’t forget to share your contributions on social media and use #PickUp3

Safety tips:

  • Be extra cautious when looking for garbage near driftwood logs, cliffs, or other potentially hazardous areas.
  • Don’t pick up any needles, unless you are an adult who has a suitable sharps container.
  • Don’t pick up any kind of medical waste, oil, oiled materials, or any other debris that could be hazardous.
  • Don’t touch any dead or injured animals. If you believe an animal is in distress, call your local authority.

Top 5 items found at beach cleanups:

  • Cigarette butts
  • Food wrappers
  • Plastic beverage bottles
  • Plastic bottle caps
  • Plastic grocery bags

Source: Ocean Conservancy
